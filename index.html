About

I'm a baseball player at New York University. I study Finance at Stern, and I’m also pursuing a minor in Computer Science.

For me, Texas is home. I was born and raised in Sugar Land, TX but moved to New York City to be surrounded by the best talent. Living in NYC, I connect with many awesome creators and personalities. Several have become my closest friends.

Much of my recent interest has involved discrete mathematics, and I'm living in Florida this summer to work in private equity. I write down some of my thoughts and experiences here.

Work

I currently work as a Summer Analyst at South Wake Capital. So far, I've learned about Merger and LBO modeling, Corporate Valuation, and Financial Statement Analysis.

In 2021, I held a business development role at Andor: a venture studio that builds companies using a unique incubation model to create, and sometimes fail, at lightning pace. At Andor, I was constantly creating, and it's also how I got into coding. I'm still great friends with the founder.

Though I don't get paid for it, I really enjoy teaching. I normally tutor friends at NYU in subjects ranging from Calculus to Financial Accounting to Computer Science. I do this frequently, and it helps me really master a subject.

Coding

I'm proficient in Java, Python, SQL, and HTML/CSS/JavaScript. I genuinely enjoy programming. I'll pick up a project from time to time, but I write most of my code in the classroom nowadays.

I built a few deep learning projects with TensorFlow/Keras. Their purposes include classifying x-ray images of lungs as healthy or having pneumonia, classifying forest cover types, and determining the likelihood a student would be admitted to college.

I created a point and figure charting tool to generate the chart for any publicly traded company over its past year, using a 3-box reversal method to filter out market noise. I also built a blackjack game.

Personal

I love playing baseball and everything it entails: from the eight hour practices to the team dinners and roadtrips. Naturally, it's what I put most of my time into.

Aside from baseball, I watch a lot of movies. Some of my favorites are Bronx Tale, Hell or High Water, and Whiplash, but I have a complete list on my Letterboxd. I'm always open to suggestions.

I think there's an incredible amount of value in creating things, whether it be physical items, content, or ideas. It's something I'm constantly working on.
